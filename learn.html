Learn by Doing

Four real scenarios with sample data. Copy the data, open the analyzer, paste it in, and see real statistics in action. No experience needed.

1 · Describe

What does customer satisfaction look like?

You surveyed 40 customers asking "How satisfied are you?" on a 1–5 scale. You also recorded their age group. Let's see the distribution and compare segments.

Try it yourself

  1. Click the button below to open the analyzer
  2. Choose Describe → "What do my responses look like?"
  3. Select "What percentage chose each answer?"
  4. Paste the Satisfaction column into the left box
  5. Paste the Age Group column into the right box to see breakdowns by segment
What to look for: Which rating is most common? Does satisfaction differ by age group? Are there any groups that stand out?

2 · Compare

Did the new checkout flow improve completion time?

You ran an A/B test: 20 users got the old checkout (Control) and 20 users got the redesigned version (Test). You measured completion time in seconds.

Try it yourself

  1. Open the analyzer and choose Compare
  2. Select "Is my A/B test result real or just noise?"
  3. Choose "A number (revenue, time on page, score)"
  4. Paste Control into the left box, Test into the right box
What to look for: Is the difference statistically significant (p < 0.05)? How large is the effect size? Would you roll out the new design based on this?

3 · Correlate

Is customer satisfaction related to spending?

You have satisfaction ratings (1–5) and monthly spend ($) for 25 customers. Do happier customers spend more?

Try it yourself

  1. Open the analyzer and choose Correlate
  2. Select "Are these two things related?"
  3. Choose "Both are numbers" → "Any pattern / Not sure"
  4. Paste Satisfaction as X and Spend as Y
What to look for: Is the correlation positive? How strong is it (close to 1 = very strong, close to 0 = weak)? Is it statistically significant?

4 · Predict

What factors predict whether a customer will churn?

You have data on 30 customers: satisfaction (1–5), number of support tickets, months as customer, and whether they churned (1) or stayed (0). What drives churn?

Try it yourself

  1. Open the analyzer and choose Predict
  2. Select "What predicts whether someone will [buy / churn / convert]?"
  3. Paste all 4 columns — the first column (Churned) is the outcome, the rest are predictors
What to look for: Which factors significantly predict churn? Is it low satisfaction, many support tickets, or being a new customer? How accurate is the model?
